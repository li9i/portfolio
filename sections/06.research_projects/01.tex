\textbf{Motivation}: Inventorying and localisation of products in large warehouses is constrained by
the accuracy, precision, and stamina of focus of human-led efforts. If RFID
tags substitute bar codes (optical, remember!), both needs may be fulfilled
with greater accuracy and in less time. If, additionally, robots replace
humans, their time may be liberated for other, more engaging/demanding
activities, and errors may diminish. The aim of project RELIEF was to provide
real-time Simultaneous Robot Localisation and Mapping of RFID tags in 3D space,
with centimeter accuracy.

The means of achieving these goals were three Autonomous Unmanned Robotic Vehicles:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Two
        \href{https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bo4lMI640DY}{Autonomous Unmanned Ground Vehicles}:
        one modified Yujin Turtlebot 2 (figure \ref{fig:relief_tb_1x4}) and one
        modified Robotnik RB1 (figure \ref{fig:relief_rb1_1x4})
  \item One
        \href{https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9YpBIaO4tgY}{Autonomous Unmanned Aerial Vehicle}:
        a modified Italdron Evo 4HSE (figure \ref{fig:relief_drone})
\end{itemize}

\section{My contribution}
I did research on methods and algorithms contained within the concept of
autonomous navigation, and on localisation with LIDAR sensors (particle
filters, pose tracking, global localisation, pose estimate error reduction,
LIDAR odometry). At the same time I was the sole developer/maintainer/handler
for everything related with the robots, especially when seen from the SW
perspective. This means integrating, configuring, and testing SLAM algorithms
(progressive mapping and simultaneous self-localisation), localisation after
SLAM, autonomous navigation; creating GUIs for simplified task execution by
third-parties; assisting PhD students to conduct experiments with robots
using special RFID equipment; integrating RFID-related algorithms onto the
robot and then making them work in tandem with the robot's packages.
